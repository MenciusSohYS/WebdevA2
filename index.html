Great Universe of Science Fiction

SCIENCE FICTION

Is a Genre fiction based on imagined future scientific or technological advances and major social or environmental changes, frequently portraying space or time travel and life on other planets. Some Genres portray hope, like Dune or The Culture while others portray nihilism and impending doom like in Hyperion or Cthuhlu Mythos. Some of my favourites would include, Warhammer 40k, Xeelee Sequence, Rememberance of Earth's past, Manifold Triology, Children of Time, Cowboy Bebop and Neon Genesis Evangelion
Three Body Problem

Is a story about humanity and nihilism, its about humanity panicking, destroying the earth and starving billions of lives to death. Recovering, advancing and later over estimating themselves against a threat they've never seen before. Its a book about nihilism and defeatism where most of humanity go through crisis in order to live. The book follows 3 people, the one who started the crisis, the one who found an unlikely solution to it and the one who ultimately goes through the crisis, learn more about them here
Warhammer 40k

40k is an absurd sci fi universe with things that do not make sense at all, but thats what makes it fun, it is a grim dark universe with inconsistent power scaling and feats. However, the lore is extremely diverse and multi level with many loveable and relatable factions as well as some straight up evil factions. No other science fiction is as popular and widely loved as warhammer 40k and it goes to show just how popular the series is. More on the other factions here
Xeelee Sequence

Xeelee Sequence is probably the bes science fiction I've ever read, it is about humanity trying to understand and rule over the universe with great difficulty, getting knocked back onto their knees repeatedly in the process. If 40k was grim dark, Xeelee Sequence is straight up evil, I would NOT want to be in the universe. No one in the Xeelee Sequence lives a particularly good life, all humans at the age of 10 are conscripted into the army and the rest is pretty much self explanatory

Some Sci Fi deal with different explanations to the Fermi Paradox which, to put it simply is the question as to why we haven't found any alien Civilisations.
